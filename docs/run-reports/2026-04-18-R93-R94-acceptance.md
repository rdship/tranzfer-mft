## Why R94's parallelization under-delivered vs design-doc projection

My design doc projected Option 1 + 2 combined would save **85–95 s per Group A service**. Actual saving: **~25 s per Group A service.** Factor of ~3.5× under projection. Probable reasons:

1. **Parallelism factor may be conservative.** If the thread pool is 4, 20 topics take ~5 rounds serial → ~25 s saving matches actual. My projection assumed pool=8–10.
2. **Broker-side serialization.** Redpanda may serialize `createTopics` calls even if invoked concurrently. The AdminClient can batch, but per-topic create is still sequential inside the broker's coordinator.
3. **Downstream effects under-counted.** The design-doc projection also assumed Option 2 (shared AdminClient) fully eliminated AdminClient creation overhead — R94 does share it, but each `createTopics` response still round-trips separately.

Either a ticket to bump the parallelism factor, or use `AdminClient.createTopics(Collection<NewTopic>)` with **all 20 topics in a single call** (single round-trip instead of 20 futures). The latter is strictly better.

## Gap analysis — what still has to ship to hit 120 s for every service

The design-doc recommended stack was 1 + 2 + 4 + 5. R94 landed **1 + 2 only** (parallelize + shared AdminClient). **4 (Spring AOT runtime) and 5 (@EntityScan narrowing) are still untouched.**

Expected residual savings once those land:

| Fix | Per-service saving | Services it closes |
|---|---:|---|
| Option 4 (Spring AOT runtime enable) | 10–15 s universal | encryption-service (needs 1 s, any tiny saving closes it); helps every service |
| Option 5 (@EntityScan narrower per service) | 5–10 s per service | Group B services (license, analytics, keystore) move under 120 s |
| Option 1.5 (bump parallelism from 4 → 10 **or** use single `createTopics` batch call) | 15–25 s additional for Group A | Group A services still at 158–163 s need this to land |

Combined projection: **Group A 120–130 s, Group B 100–115 s, Group C ≤ 110 s.** Still marginal for Group A — may need Option 7 (`@Lazy` on non-critical controllers) for the last 5–15 s.

## Recommendation for the dev team

Three targeted follow-ons in priority order:

1. **Land Option 4 (Spring AOT runtime)** — single JVM flag, universal 10–15 s saving, no risk. Likely closes encryption-service and pushes several Group B services under 120 s.
2. **Revisit R94's parallelism factor.** Either bump to 10, or call `AdminClient.createTopics(all20TopicsAtOnce)` as a single future. Should recover the remaining 60 s of savings my design doc projected but didn't land.
3. **Per-service @EntityScan narrowing** for the 4 slowest Group B services (license, analytics, notification, platform-sentinel) — targeted 5–10 s each.

If 1+2 land together, Group A services drop from 158–163 s to ~95–115 s. **Target met.**
